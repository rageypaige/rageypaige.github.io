Introduction

Trip Ninja is a travel technology company who offers a sophisticated API and front-end platform to help travel agencies make the most out of their flights conent. I was responsible for user research, future product ideation, user interface design, and product marketing.

QuickTrip

A lot of the work that I did for Trip Ninja was for their travel agent facing platform, QuickTrip. QuickTrip is for travel agents who want to use our technology but don't want to integrate an API.

As the product designer, I collaborated with developers and product managers to assess project outcomes and prioritize future features. I also designed user flows, wireframes, and conducted usability testing for those features.

The following designs are from the larger projects I worked on.

QuickTrip is designed to be the one-stop-shop for travel agents. They can do all of the traditional activities of travel agents such as search and book flights for their clients, but we have designed many additional features that help them grow their travel business.

The admin panel allows travel agents to manage their business, customize their search results, and track their progress. I created the user flows from user research, designed wireframes, and designed final UI designs for this project.

Web Design

The product and sales team collaborated on a redesign of the website. We wanted to take a new approach to highlight our technology and stand out.

I worked closely with the Sales Director and CEO to create a fresh and modern design.

This is a sample of the Homepage that we designed. In total, we designed a total of 7 pages.

Once we launched the redesign of the site, we saw an increase of booked demos and overall site traffic.

Branding

I also led a rebrand of Trip Ninja. The goal was to align our visual and verbal communication with the core values that drive Trip Ninja: authenticity, innovation, and connection.

We introduced a refined color palette, modern typography, and a clean design system to create consistency and establish a memorable impression across all touchpoints. This wasn't just about aesthetics but about ensuring that every interaction felt intentional and cohesive.

A crucial part of this journey was collaboration. By engaging stakeholders and listening to customer feedback, I ensured the rebrand resonated with both internal teams and our audience. The result? A brand that not only looks fresh but also resonates on a deeper level, embodying who Trip Ninja is and where they are headed.
