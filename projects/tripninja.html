Desktop Portal Designs

A lot of the work that I do for Trip Ninja is on their travel agent facing platform, QuickTrip. QuickTrip is for travel agents who want to use our technology but don't want to integrate an API.

As the product designer, I collaborate with developers and product managers to assess project outcomes and prioritize future features. I also design user flows, wireframes, and conduct usability testing for these features.

The following designs are from the larger projects I worked on.

Admin Panel

QuickTrip is designed to be the one-stop-shop for travel agents. They can do all of the traditional activities of travel agents such as search and book flights for their clients, but we have designed many additional features that help them grow their travel business.

The admin panel allows travel agents to manage their business, customize their search results, and track their progress. I created the user flows, wireframes, and final UI designs for this project.

Multi Stop Dynamic Packaging

MSDP provides an alternative to traditional, static packaging. It allows users to build packages as they go; while they're searching for multi-destination flights, MSDP will find eligible hotels with which to bundle and enjoy discounts (likewise from a hotels search perspective).

Very few, if any, travel companies have developed this product despite a large demand for it. Seen here are the initial user flow mapping that I did.
